I quite like Patum Peperium Gentleman’s Relish, and I don’t mind who knows it. In fact, it’s probably my favourite of all the spiced anchovy relishes.

When I began writing this, its Wikipedia entry still had a strong “In popular culture” section. Since then, some po-faced monster has removed it, although of course you can still read the old version. If I were a true Gentleman’s Relish fan, I would start some kind of “edit war”, but I haven’t which I suppose proves I’m not one.

Whereas some other anchovy pastes come in tubes – easily confusable for tomato purée or toothpaste – Gentleman’s Relish comes in a pot. On the label is written “Use very sparingly”. Tom Parker Bowles writes in the Daily Mail that “In an age where excess consumption is not just endured but encouraged, this comes as a breath of fresh air.” Parker Bowles may approve, but it always makes me worry am I using sparingly enough? So it’s a comfort that the picture accompanying the article shows some relish being used not very sparingly at all, far less sparingly than I would ever allow.

For those of us who aren’t the son of the heir presumptive to the Earldom of Macclesfield – or maybe especially for those of us who are, because aren’t aristocrats increasingly penurious people living in crumbly houses? – an important question is what the most relish I can buy for the least money? The following table shows the prices and weights of the pots available from what I think are the three largest Gentleman’s Relish–stocking retail chains, correct at the time of publication according to mySupermarket.co.uk:
