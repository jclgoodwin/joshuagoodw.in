I have a Braun AB5 (Type 4748) alarm clock. I think it’s one of the original ones, and not the modern revival (the BNC001), which is not to say I think one is better than the other or anything.

The Silicon Valley blowhard Dustin Curtis has a BNC002 – a modern revivial of a slightly different Braun alarm clock – which he reckons is “the best”. It’s not clear if he’s investigated ones produced before Dieter Rams entered his dotage and took up bonsai gardening, or necessarily. I think the more circular rounder shape,  ((The other essay in that series, in which he bleats cutlery, is a bit more contemptible.))

I unders has “German Movement” written on its face, which shows a suspiciously fierce keenness to insist that it’s definitely not inferior to the original model.1 My supposedly original AB5 isn’t brilliant – it makes a quite audible ticking noise, and features the same horrible rhapsody-of-beeps alarm sound as every other commodity alarm clock I’ve met.

Recently, my AB5 became a bit broken. What I will call the alarm hand no longer correctly indicated the actual time at which the alarm would sound; and the hour and minute hands had become misaligned such that when the minute hand was pointing at 12 (at what I think they call the top of the hour), the hour hand was not pointing directly at any number. Neither catastrophic problems, but both annoying, so I wondered how to fix them.
